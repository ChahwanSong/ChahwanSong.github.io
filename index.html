Research Projects

DySO : revisiting application offloads on programmable switches

Application offloads on modern high-speed programmable switches have been proposed in a variety of systems (e.g., key-value store systems and network middleboxes) so as to efficiently scale up the traditional server-oriented deployments. However, they largely achieve sub-optimal offloading efficiency due to the lack of (1) capability to perform control actions at sufficient rates, and (2) adaptability to workload changes.
In this project, we scrutinize the common stumbling blocks of existing frameworks with performance evaluations on real workloads. We present DySO (Dynamic State Offloading), a framework which enables expeditious on-demand control actions and self-tuning of management rules. Our software simulations show up to 100% performance improvement compared to existing systems for various real world traces. On top of that, we implement and evaluate DySO on a commodity programmable switch, showing two orders of magnitude faster responsiveness to sudden workload changes compared to the existing systems.

FCM-Sketch : lightweight data structure for network measurements

We observe that recent sketches for network measurements consume lots of hardware resources or even unavailable on programmable switches. Typically, most existing data-plane apps use Count-Min sketch by its simplicity albeit its poor accuracy as a trade-off.
We propose FCM-Sketch, a probabilistic data structure supporting accurate and generic network measurements such as flow size, cardinality, flow size distribution and entropy. FCM-Sketch has a lightweight implementation on commodity programmable switches (Barefoot Tofino), and we expect FCM-Sketch will be used as a substitute for Count-Min in future data-plane applications.
